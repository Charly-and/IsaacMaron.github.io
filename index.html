Mechatronics 2024

Automated Multi-Material Sorting System

Designed and programmed a competitive sorting system capable of classifying and routing 48 mixed pieces — aluminium, steel, black and white nylon — in under 20 seconds. The system combined inductive proximity sensing for metal detection with an optical contrast sensor for polymer classification, all orchestrated through custom microcontroller logic driving stepper motors, PWM conveyor control, and an end-gate actuator.

Thermofluids 2026

Minifridge Reverse Engineering & COP Optimisation

Conducted a full reverse-engineering analysis of a commercial minifridge to diagnose poor thermal performance and reduce duty cycle. Wrote an iterative Python model using CoolProp to simulate the refrigeration cycle — coupling energy balances, mass flow convergence, and the Lockhart-Martinelli pressure-drop correlation in the condenser. The model accurately predicted the elevated steady-state interior temperatures (~9 °C above spec) reported by end users, validating the approach before proposing design changes.

Key recommendations: increased condenser tube length, improved door and wall insulation, enlarged evaporator area. Novel proposal: desiccant wheel driven by condenser waste heat (55 °C) to provide zero-operating-cost dehumidification — predicted to reduce duty cycle by 8–15% by suppressing evaporator frost accumulation.
Python CoolProp Thermodynamic modelling Lockhart-Martinelli Two-phase flow
Mechanism Design 2024

Motorised Retractable Bridge — MECH 350 Design Project

Designed, analysed, and built a retractable bridge constrained to an 11-inch height envelope, spanning 32 inches and clearing 22 inches on retraction while supporting a 2 kg live load with vehicle passage. The selected design uses a reverse double four-bar linkage with a 1:1 gear-coupled central chassis, ensuring symmetric, predictable extension with minimal angular error. Retraction is powered by a DC motor driving a custom gear reduction to a cable-spool system routed through eyelets on the floating chassis for maximum mechanical advantage at full extension.

Structure fabricated from 6 mm birch plywood with laser-cut gear stack; pivots on steel shafts with 3D-printed retaining pins. Controlled via bidirectional switch for extend, hold, and retract.
Four-bar linkage Gear design Laser cutting 3D printing DC motor Cable drive

Designed a three-stage compound gear train for a fictional electric vehicle platform (Nissan Blade) with a 12:1 speed reduction target, constrained to 160% of the Nissan Leaf transmission volume.
